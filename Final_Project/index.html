Data Privacy

What is data privacy?

Data privacy is the protection of an individual’s data, including sensitive personal information, interaction tracking, biometric data tracking, geolocation tracking, etc.


Why is data privacy important?

As our world becomes exponentially digitized, we leap further into the cyborg and away from a purely human experience. Cyborg? Yes, you read right. Think about it— a lot of our interactions are not purely physical; we work and take classes remotely, a good portion of our social lives are on social media platforms, we shop, sell, and are exposed to advertisements online, and even our healthcare is digitized through virtual care. A good portion of our lives have moved onto digital platforms, making our lives easier in many ways, but it has been at the expense of something we’ve taken for granted— our privacy.

How real are the threats to my data privacy?

A lot of people underestimate how vulnerable we are in terms of the information we provide online. Studies have shown that the amount of technical knowledge someone has about data privacy does not correlate with the measures taken to protect oneself from privacy threats. Instead, let’s see how real data breaches are for the average individual.


Breaches by Education— Insights

The average individual, regardless of level of education, has experienced at least one data breach. The post graduate respondents seem to face more data breaches than the other groups, with the average post graduate experiencing at least two data breaches.

Breaches by Income— Insights

The average individual, regardless of income, experiences at least one data breach, with the individual average being closer to two for upper class individuals. Although the lower income group has a lower individual average for total breaches, the number of breaches across income groups is quite close, falling betwen 1.6 and 2.

Breaches by Age— Insights

The radius of the circles are proportionate to the relative frequency of individuals at that age and who has experienced that specified number of breaches. A total breach count of at one spreads across all age groups, and the number of breaches tends to be higher for middle aged individuals. The frequency of individuals is high among all age groups at 0-2 total breaches, and begins tapering at higher numbers of total breaches.

Conclusion

It's apparent that the threats to data privacy do not discriminate. We are all vulnerable and need to take measures to protect ourselves by pressuring legislation to establish more explicit information protection policies for consumers. The need for protection is ever-increasing as more and more of our data is collected through our digital experiences.
